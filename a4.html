Architectural Design Principles Roadmap

Balance

Creating visual stability by distributing elements evenly.

  • Symmetrical Balance – Mirror-like repetition.
  • Asymmetrical Balance – Different elements, visually balanced.
  • Radial Balance – Arranged around a central point.

Example: Taj Mahal, Parthenon.

Reference Book: "Architecture: Form, Space, and Order" by Francis D.K. Ching

Proportion & Scale

Ensuring proper size relationships between elements.

  • Golden Ratio – 1:1.618, used for perfect proportions.
  • Human Scale – Proportions comfortable for humans.

Example: The Parthenon in Greece.

Reference Book: "The Elements of Modern Architecture" by Antony Radford

Rhythm & Repetition

Creating a sense of movement through repeated elements.

  • Alternation – Changing patterns (e.g., different windows).
  • Progression – Increasing or decreasing size.

Example: Gothic cathedrals (arches & columns).

Reference Book: "Architectural Graphics" by Francis D.K. Ching

Unity & Harmony

All elements work together to create a cohesive design.

  • Consistent color, materials, and design language.
  • Repeating shapes and themes.

Example: Sydney Opera House.

Reference Book: "Architecture: Form, Space, and Order" by Francis D.K. Ching

Light & Shadow

Using natural and artificial light to enhance design.

  • Natural Light – Windows, skylights, daylight control.
  • Artificial Light – Interior lighting for ambiance.

Example: Le Corbusier’s Ronchamp Chapel.

Reference Book: "Architectural Lighting Design" by Gary Steffy
